Project Update: Meeting The Team

Building a Safer Community Through Technology
Welcome to my first weekly blog post regarding my final capstone project. For this project, I am focusing on a problem that affects many people in our local neighborhoods: the loss or theft of personal property. It can be very difficult for a person to find their belongings once they are gone. My goal is to build a community-based web application that allows people to search for missing or stolen items. By using Python and artificial intelligence, this app will help neighbors work together to recover their property more quickly.

Team Collaboration and Planning
This week was very productive for my team. We met for the first time to get to know one another and discuss our strengths. It is important for a team to be on the same page before starting a big project. To help guide us, I spent a lot of time creating our Software Design Document (SDD). This document is like a master plan for our software. It explains the technical parts of the app and how the code will be organized. Having a strong plan is a key step in professional software development.

Visual Design and Technology
While I worked on the technical plan, my teammate, Eric, led the creation of our Style Tile. This document shows the colors, fonts, and buttons we will use. It ensures our app looks professional and is easy for people to use. We have also officially decided on our "tech stack." We will be using Python as our main language. This choice is perfect for our project because Python is great for data science and AI features. We are excited to move forward with a clear vision and the right tools.
